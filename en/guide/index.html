About
Busan National Gugak Center

Busan National Gugak Center was established on October 28 2008 in order to preserve and promote the traditional performing arts of Busan․Yeongnam region. It aims to contribute in developing arts and culture by creating traditions with local sentiment.
The Center, on the basis of Busan as a center place in Asia Pacific region, also plans to raise the dignity of the city and to spread Korean traditional culture to the world by designing new shows and concerts.

Gugak Performance Groups

Traditional Music Orchestra/
Traditional Vocal Music Group

Traditional Music Orchestra and Traditional Vocal Music Group consisted of more than fifty artists who are the best musicians with great artistic ability. The director as well as the members endeavor to continue Korea’s tradition to the future by performing all kinds of music, including old elegant music, folk music, and creative music. Especially, the group puts effort to preserve and to transmit uniqueness of Yeongnam arts by making high quality performance, so that the modern audience understands it. At the same time, the group tries that the music survives and stays around the people’s life, not only in Korea but also in all the global society.

Dance Theater

Including the director and choreographers, the thirty members in Dance Theater are the best artists in Korea with great ability. They perform all types of Korean dance, from court festive dance to folk and creative dances, in order to transmit and to develop the traditional dance heritage. Moreover, it tries to introduce and to promote Yeongnam dances which are characterized as being active and stylish. With the bodily movements, the dancers are now making performances that can communicate with audience.

Facilities

Yeonak-dang

The main theater with 686 seats (including eight seats for disabled) and a proscenium stage sized in 1,258㎡. The hall symbolizes a traditional instrument, ‘Yonggo’ the dragon drum, and the its proscenium arch in the theater show modernized Korean architecture with its traditional wooden styling. The hall is suitable for many types of show in large scale and multi-dimensional with its convertible stage that move rotating, elevating, and sliding sideward. Moreover, the hall is equipped with high-quality technology of LED lighting system capable of presenting colorful and beautiful performances, along with UHD(4K) video camera and 4k laser beam projector.

Yeji-dang

The proscenium small hall designed for natural sound system with 276 seats (including 3 wheelchair seats) and a stage sized in 231㎡. The building looks like a traditional house with its stone fence wall and wooden floors, so that the audience might feel like seeing the performance in their cozy chamber room, even sensing the breath of the musicians. The hall is designed suitable for acoustic sound so the audience can feel the sound of traditional instruments that are made of all natural materials. In addition, the artists with creative works can express their modern sensibility well with its proper sound system and environments.

Education Experience Center

Teaching Room

There are total eight class rooms including two large (266㎡), four medium (79㎡), and two small (64㎡) rooms in the building. All of these spaces are equipped with good sound proof system and thus different music programs can be run in each room all at the same time. All the rooms are wooden floor suitable for instrument learning sitting on the floor. Instruments are provided for the program participants who are willing to learn the music.

Digital Exhibition Space

Digital Exhibition Space is located in the exhibition room No. 2 (B1) and the exhibition room No. 4 (1st floor). with the collections about traditional music in Yeongnam region. The materials are all digitalized and provided to the visitors. The room No. 2 prepares interactive books and media table for an easy and exciting learning of Yoengnam culture. On the other hand, the room No. 4 provides academic and professional materials on a large size archiving wall and media art.

Tangible and Realistic Contents Experience Room

Tangible and Realistic Contents Experience Room is located in the exhibition room No. 1 (B1) and No. 3 (1st floor). The room combines traditional music and the state of art IT technology and provides the visitors an opportunity of multi dimensional and immersive experience. The room No. 1 provides a fantastic immersive realistic video featuring local dance of Yeongnam region, such as Dongrae crane dance and Jinju sword dance. The room No. 3 provides a funny interactive experience preparing masks used in local mask theater.

Convenient facilities

Book Cafe

Book Cafe is a complex cultural space open to all the visitors. It is also a library of academic books for the education of and research on traditional music, filled with more than one thousand books on culture and arts as well as some general books. Any one can freely use the library and read books. Moreover, the library provides music files downloaded in the library's computer, and thus the visitors can listen to various tunes, from the old records to modern composition. The time of operation is nine am to six pm from Tuesday to Saturday. The venue closes on Sundays and holidays.
